Your portfolio health score is calculated from 5 dimensions: Diversification (25%), Risk Management (25%), Performance (20%), Tax Efficiency (15%), and Income (15%). Scores are intentionally conservative — a score of 70+ is genuinely good. Each dimension evaluates specific metrics like position concentration (HHI index), geographic spread, win/loss ratios, account type placement, and income allocation relative to your risk profile. Geographic Exposure

Analyze your portfolio's geographic diversification and currency exposure. Canadian investors often exhibit "home bias" — overweighting domestic securities despite Canada representing only ~3% of global market capitalization. A well-diversified portfolio typically includes exposure to US, international developed, and emerging markets.
